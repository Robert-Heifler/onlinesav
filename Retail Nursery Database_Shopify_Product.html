Nursery Sales and Inventory Database (Microsoft Access)

Nursery Sales and Inventory Database is a working Microsoft Access database built for plant nurseries and garden centers that sell live plants and supplies. It helps you run the front counter, track inventory, manage deliveries, and print the reports you need without spreadsheets or handwritten notes.

What it manages

  • Customers with searchable contact info (name, contact, phone, email, city, state, postal code, preferred contact)
  • Products organized by supplier and category, with SKU, plant type, variety, size text, unit of measure, price, cost, perennial flag, quantity on hand, and reorder point
  • Sales orders with order date, requested date, payment method, and payment status
  • Sales order lines with quantity, unit price, discounts, tax, subtotals, totals, and backorder tracking

Reports included (17 total): Backorders, Customer Directory, Delivery List, Inventory Value, Order Header, Order Detail, Order Lines, Product Catalog, Reorder List, Sales by Category, Sales by Customer, Sales by Payment Method, Sales by Payment Status, Top Customers, Top Products, plus print templates.

Why nurseries use it

  • Reduce lost sales by spotting unfilled backorders and acting before customers walk away
  • Buy smarter by seeing which SKUs are under reorder and how many units you are short
  • Protect margin by understanding inventory retail value, cost value, and gross margin value by supplier and category
  • Speed up daily work with prompts that filter reports by date range, customer, supplier, category, product, state, payment method, and payment status
  • Print a clean delivery list by requested date range with full shipping details

Requirements: Microsoft Access (desktop) or the free Microsoft Access Runtime. All tables, queries, and reports are editable, so you can add fields, change labels, and tailor layouts to your workflow.

Ideal for: small nurseries, garden centers, and wholesale growers with a retail counter that need a straightforward way to track orders, inventory, payments, and reporting in one place.

Getting started: add your customers and product list, set reorder points and on-hand quantities, then begin entering sales orders. Run reports anytime and enter the prompted filters to narrow results. Most users are productive the same day, and the structure makes it easy to expand as you add new categories, suppliers, or locations.

Note: This is a desktop database for Microsoft Access users. No monthly fees.

Download the free Microsoft Access Runtime if you don't have Microsoft Access installed.
