AI Database Builder — Free vs Upgrade FAQ

What you can do with the Free Edition and what changes when you enter the upgrade key.

Q1. What do I get in the Free Edition?

The Free Edition lets you generate a complete working database structure. You can create unlimited tables and up to six forms or reports. All automation features run normally so you can see exactly how the tool works. Start entering your information in the forms and view them with reports.

Q2. What happens when I hit the free limit?

When you try to create a seventh form or report, the tool stops and shows an upgrade message. Nothing is deleted — you simply cannot generate new forms or reports until you enter your upgrade key.

Q3. If I download the Free Edition again, will it reset the limit?

No. Re-downloading the same Free Edition gives you the same limits. Each copy tracks its own usage inside the file. You can always start over with a new copy, but you will hit the same free limit again. The fastest way to keep working is to unlock your existing copy with your key.

Q4. What does the upgrade key do?

Entering your upgrade key instantly unlocks unlimited form and report generation in that copy of the tool. It also enables a special button to export your entire project into a new editable ACCDB file.

That exported ACCDB contains all your generated tables, queries, forms, and reports, ready to open directly in Microsoft Access. Once exported, you (or any developer you choose) can open it in Access and edit any form or report in Design View, adjust layouts, add fields, or build new functionality.

You only need to enter your upgrade key once; it stays unlocked for future use.

Q5. How much does the upgrade cost?

A one-time payment of US $39 unlocks all features for that copy of the tool. No subscriptions, no online activation, and no tracking.

Visit this upgrade page to purchase an unlock key. You will receive your key immediately after checkout.

Q7. Why not just keep using the Free Edition?

The Free Edition is ideal for initial design and testing. Once you start creating more forms or reports or want to move your work into a standalone, editable database, upgrading becomes the simplest way to keep everything together and fully customizable.

Most users upgrade quickly because it saves time and lets them own, edit, and extend their Access database without limits.

Hiring a Microsoft Access developer typically costs between $50 and $120 per hour. Even a small setup with the same number of forms or reports allowed in the free edition would usually cost $300–$700 in professional fees. With this builder, the process is automated, consistent, and repeatable — no hourly billing. If you ever need customization beyond the included automation, there are inexpensive options available.
